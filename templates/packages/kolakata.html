What to Do in Kolkata

Explore the historic landmarks like Victoria Memorial and Howrah Bridge.

Visit the iconic Dakshineswar Kali Temple and Belur Math.

Stroll through the bustling streets of College Street, the hub of bookstores.

Experience the artistic and intellectual vibe at the Indian Museum and Tagore’s house.

Enjoy the scenic beauty of the Hooghly River with a boat ride at Prinsep Ghat.

Savor delicious Bengali cuisine, including rasgulla, mishti doi, and fish curry.

Shop for traditional Bengali sarees in markets like Gariahat and New Market.

Attend the grand Durga Puja festival, Kolkata’s biggest cultural celebration.

Watch a cricket match or football game at Eden Gardens, one of India’s largest stadiums.

Relax in the greenery of Maidan and Rabindra Sarobar.

About Kolkata

Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, is a city rich in history, culture, and artistic heritage. Known as the "City of Joy," it is famous for its colonial architecture, intellectual traditions, and vibrant festivals. Iconic landmarks such as the Howrah Bridge, Victoria Memorial, and Dakshineswar Kali Temple showcase the city's architectural grandeur.
