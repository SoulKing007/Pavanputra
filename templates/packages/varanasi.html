About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, situated along the banks of the Ganges River in northern India. Renowned as a major religious hub, it holds profound significance in Hindu traditions of pilgrimage, death, and mourning. The city's rich cultural heritage is evident in its historic ghats, temples, and vibrant arts scene.
